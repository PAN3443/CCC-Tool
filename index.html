Manage Your Colormaps

Welcome to the CCC-Tool. The following is a short instruduction. For a comprehensive introduction please visit the tutorial. The present page is the My Maps side, where you can manage your set of colormaps and handle the available options for the colormaps. You can reach this window everytime by clicking on the "My Maps" button at the right top corner. For a easy and fast start you can choose the option of adding an existing colormap (click on the "Add Existing Map" button). The new displayed side show you a set of common used colormaps. Your selection will automatically tranfer to this side, where you can customize these maps. Alternative to the selction of a existing colormap you can create your own colormap, which meet your special requirements (click on the "Create New Map" button). If you need help at the creator side please hover the questionmark button at the top for a short help or visit the tutorial. Start with:

Colormap Course

HELP: With the analyse of the colormap course, you can see how the colormap run to the hue space and how the colormap changes the lightness (brightness) value.

With the course in the hue space you can see how close together the band courses are or if they are crossing. For a good colormap the band courses shouldn’t run to close together and they shouldn't cross. Inside the hue space the course of a scaled band is shown with a solid line with a circle at the start- and end-color. (Info:: You can modify the position of a circle with clicking and pressing the mousbotton above the circle and subsequent a move to the new position.) Constant Bands are shown as a quad and can also modify like the circles of the scaled bands. The dashed lines show you the jumps between two bands with different neighboring colors. They are not part of the colormap and they do not take into account for the analysis.

The course of the colormap through the brightness is also an important part for the analyse. Inside the plot for the brightness course you can evaluate the in- or decrease of the brightness. For neighboring band colors it could be also important to have a maximum or a minimus of difference.
